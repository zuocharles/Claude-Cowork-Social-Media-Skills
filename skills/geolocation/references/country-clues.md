## Bollards

Bollards are one of the most reliable country identifiers — 95%+ accuracy when visible.

### Europe
| Country | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **France** | White rounded post, distinctive red reflective strip at top. Unique in Europe. |
| **Poland** | Red diagonal stripe wrapping around the bollard. |
| **Czech Republic** | Fluorescent orange stripes in the black section. Back has white rectangle in black. |
| **Hungary** | Red reflector on front, white reflector on back. |
| **Austria** | Black or dark red reflectors on front, distinctive "black hat" on top. No other country has both features. |
| **Germany** | Rectangular reflectors, white front + red back. Often tall and narrow. |
| **Netherlands** | Red/white striped posts, sometimes with orange reflectors. |
| **Italy** | Often red with white reflective strip, varies by region. |
| **Spain** | Cylindrical with red/white bands or just white with red reflector. |
| **Sweden** | Short white posts with yellow reflector tops. |
| **Norway** | Similar to Sweden but sometimes with orange reflectors. |
| **Finland** | Yellow-tipped white posts, very similar to Scandinavian neighbors. |
| **Romania** | White post with red back reflectors, similar to Hungary but distinct shape. |
| **Bulgaria** | White with blue reflective strips. |
| **Croatia** | White with red reflective sections, rounder than German style. |
| **Slovakia** | Similar to Czech but without the fluorescent orange. Uses green reflectors sometimes. |

### Rest of World
| Country | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Japan** | Flexible delineator posts, often orange or green. Urban areas use small stone bollards. |
| **South Korea** | Bright yellow or orange flexible posts. |
| **Australia** | Flexible guide posts, white with red reflectors (left side) or white reflectors (right side). |
| **New Zealand** | Similar to Australia, sometimes with yellow. |
| **Brazil** | Concrete or plastic, often yellow/black striped. |
| **Argentina** | Metal with reflective paint, often weathered. |

## Utility Poles

### Key Identifiers
| Country/Region | Distinguishing Feature |
|----------------|----------------------|
| **France** | Small blue stickers on poles — exclusive to France within Europe, highly reliable. |
| **Poland** | "Holy poles" — holes only in the middle section of the pole. Distinctive pattern. |
| **Japan** | Extremely dense wiring, multiple crossarms, often with transformers visible. Urban spaghetti. |
| **United States** | Wooden poles with single or double crossarms. Standardized but varied by region. |
| **Russia** | Concrete poles, often in poor condition. Thick and utilitarian. |
| **India** | Concrete or steel, often overloaded with wires, sometimes leaning. |
| **Latin America** | Wooden poles, often without uniform standards, sometimes visibly improvised connections. |
| **Scandinavia** | Clean, minimal wiring. Often metal poles in urban areas. |
| **Sub-Saharan Africa** | Wooden poles, sparse wiring, often spaced far apart. |

## License Plates

### Europe
All EU/EEA countries have a **blue strip on the left** with the country code:
- D = Germany, F = France, I = Italy, E = Spain, P = Portugal
- NL = Netherlands, B = Belgium, A = Austria, CH = Switzerland (red strip, not blue)
- PL = Poland, CZ = Czech Republic, SK = Slovakia, H = Hungary
- RO = Romania, BG = Bulgaria, HR = Croatia, SLO = Slovenia

**UK**: Post-Brexit plates may or may not have the blue strip. Shape is wider than EU standard.

### Americas
| Region | Notes |
|--------|-------|
| **US** | Varies by state. Rectangular, diverse designs. Front plate not required in all states. |
| **Canada** | Similar size to US. Province-specific designs. |
| **Mexico** | Rectangular, state-specific, often colorful. |
| **Brazil** | Mercosul format (white with blue band on top) since 2018. |
| **Argentina** | Also Mercosul format. |

### Asia
| Country | Notes |
|---------|-------|
| **Japan** | Small, rectangular. Yellow for kei cars (<660cc), white for regular. Green text. |
| **South Korea** | Wider format. Green plates for commercial, white for private. |
| **China** | Blue plates (most common), green for EVs. Chinese characters + letters. |
| **India** | White front plate, yellow rear plate. Oblong shape. |
| **Thailand** | Various colors by vehicle type. Thai script. |

### Middle East / Africa
| Region | Notes |
|--------|-------|
| **Saudi Arabia** | Arabic + English text. Distinctive format. |
| **UAE** | Varies by emirate. Often with emirate name in Arabic + English. |
| **South Africa** | Province-coded. Various formats by province era. |
| **Kenya** | White plates, "K" prefix. |
| **Nigeria** | White with green stripe, state codes. |

## Road Markings

| Feature | Countries |
|---------|-----------|
| **White center line** | Most of Europe, Asia, Oceania |
| **Yellow center line** | US, Canada, most of Latin America |
| **Double middle lines** | Poland (distinctive), also some other countries |
| **Rumble strips on center** | Scandinavia, parts of US |
| **Red road edges** | Netherlands (cycle path markings nearby) |
| **Blue road signs for highways** | Most of Europe |
| **Green road signs for highways** | US, Brazil, parts of Asia |
| **No center markings at all** | Common in rural developing countries |

## Driving Side

### Left-hand traffic (drive on the left)
UK, Ireland, Japan, Australia, New Zealand, India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, Sri Lanka, Thailand,
Indonesia, Malaysia, Singapore, Hong Kong, South Africa, Kenya, Tanzania, Uganda, Mozambique,
Botswana, Zimbabwe, Jamaica, Trinidad & Tobago, Barbados, Cyprus, Malta, Suriname, Timor-Leste

### Right-hand traffic (everywhere else)
All of continental Europe, all of the Americas (except above), all of the Middle East,
China, South Korea, Russia, most of Africa, most of Southeast Asia (except above)

**Key trick**: All of South America drives on the right. All of North America drives on the right.
If you see left-hand traffic, you're almost certainly in a former British colony, Japan, Thailand,
or Indonesia.

## Google Street View Meta

### Camera Generations
| Generation | Characteristics | Common In |
|------------|----------------|-----------|
| **Gen 1** | Low resolution, washed out colors, visible seams | Early coverage (2007-2008). US, France, parts of Australia. |
| **Gen 2** | Better resolution, still some artifacts | 2009-2012 era. Expanded European coverage. |
| **Gen 3** | Good quality, natural colors | 2012-2017. Most global expansion. |
| **Gen 4** | High resolution, sharp, vibrant colors | 2017+. Latest coverage in most countries. |

### Google Car Variations
| Country | Car/Meta Detail |
|---------|----------------|
| **Ghana** | Roof bars with black tape |
| **Kenya** | Car with a snorkel (for river crossings) |
| **Nigeria** | Often followed by a police escort car |
| **Mongolia** | Frequently a white SUV with visible side mirrors |
| **Albania** | Blue/gray Google car with different roof setup |
| **Senegal** | Trekker (backpack camera) coverage in many areas |
| **Bhutan** | Very limited coverage, often trekker-based |

### Coverage Patterns
- **Full coverage**: US, most of Europe, Japan, South Korea, Australia, Brazil, South Africa
- **Partial/urban only**: Much of Africa, Central Asia, Middle East
- **No official coverage**: China (uses Baidu/Tencent instead), North Korea, most of Central Africa

## Scripts and Languages

### Quick Script Identification
| Script | Countries |
|--------|-----------|
| **Latin (no diacritics)** | English-speaking countries, Dutch, Malay/Indonesian |
| **Latin (with ñ)** | Spanish-speaking countries |
| **Latin (with ç, ã, õ)** | Portugal, Brazil |
| **Latin (with ø, å)** | Denmark, Norway |
| **Latin (with ä, ö, ü)** | Germany, Austria, Switzerland, Turkey (also ş, ğ, ı) |
| **Latin (with ą, ę, ł, ź)** | Poland |
| **Latin (with ř, ě, ů)** | Czech Republic |
| **Cyrillic** | Russia, Ukraine, Belarus, Bulgaria, Serbia, North Macedonia, Mongolia |
| **Cyrillic with ъ** | Bulgaria (common word endings) |
| **Cyrillic with ї, є, ґ** | Ukraine |
| **Hangul** | South Korea (North Korea has no Street View) |
| **Kanji + Hiragana + Katakana** | Japan |
| **Simplified Chinese** | China (mainland) |
| **Traditional Chinese** | Taiwan, Hong Kong |
| **Thai script** | Thailand (curvy, with many tonal markers) |
| **Khmer script** | Cambodia (very ornate, rounded) |
| **Lao script** | Laos (similar to Thai but rounder) |
| **Myanmar script** | Myanmar (circular/loopy characters) |
| **Devanagari** | India (Hindi), Nepal |
| **Tamil script** | Southern India, Sri Lanka, Singapore |
| **Arabic script** | Middle East, North Africa, Pakistan, Iran |
| **Arabic with extra dots** | Urdu (Pakistan) — additional characters like ٹ, ڈ, ے |
| **Ge'ez/Ethiopic** | Ethiopia, Eritrea |
| **Georgian script** | Georgia (unique rounded alphabet) |
| **Armenian script** | Armenia (unique alphabet) |

## Miscellaneous

### Satellite Dishes
Dishes point toward geostationary satellites over the equator.
- Dishes pointing **south** = Northern Hemisphere
- Dishes pointing **north** = Southern Hemisphere
- The steepness of the angle indicates latitude (steeper = closer to equator)

### Phone Number Formats on Signs
- +1 = US/Canada
- +44 = UK
- +33 = France
- +49 = Germany
- +55 = Brazil
- +91 = India
- +81 = Japan
- +86 = China

| **Petrobras / BR** | Brazil |
| **Shell** | Global, but dominant in Netherlands, UK, Nigeria |

### Trash Cans and Street Furniture
- Region-specific trash can designs are searchable via Overpass Turbo (OpenStreetMap)
- Fire hydrant styles vary by country and are surprisingly distinctive
- Postbox colors: Red = UK, Royal Mail | Yellow = Germany, Deutsche Post | Blue = US, USPS | Green = Ireland, An Post

### Domain Extensions on Billboards
- .co.uk = UK, .com.br = Brazil, .de = Germany, .fr = France
- .co.za = South Africa, .com.au = Australia, .co.jp = Japan
- .ru = Russia, .pl = Poland, .nl = Netherlands
